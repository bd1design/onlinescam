What Are Online Trading Scams?

You could consider the ‘online trading scam’ an umbrella term for a wide array of schemes and frauds that are quite frequent on the internet. Fraudulent organizations tend to churn out a large number of low-quality sites that act as money traps. This way they are casting a wider web to catch victims. However, they also make high-quality deceptions that can steal funds from seasoned and cautious investors.

The common aspect of these scams is that they offer unrealistic investment returns through trading. They could come in the form of broker platforms that offer trading conditions that are too good to be true. Such platforms come with various types of baits, so they might promote forex trading, binary options, crypto exchange, etc. Additionally, exceptional trading plans and magical trading bots are also a common hook for Ponzi schemes and investment scams. This just proves the point that investors need to be very careful when they are looking for a trading opportunity online.

CRYPTOCURRENCY & BITCOIN SCAM

Financial experts and trading enthusiasts have recognized the huge potential for earnings in the turbulent economy of the cryptocurrency frontier. Sadly, they aren’t the only ones. In recent years, there has been a huge increase in scams that are after people’s Bitcoins and altcoins.

Most commonly, scammers set up fake crypto trading platforms with exceptional bonuses and benefits. They make all kinds of sweet deals, to entice the target to invest in their crypto trap. There are also numerous traps in the forms of Ponzi schemes with crypto payments; unsecured e-wallets that supposedly generate income; investing opportunities in worthless altcoins that are prone to pump and dump moves, and the list goes on.

Forex Scam

Forex trading (aka foreign exchange) is a legitimate discipline of generating funds in which an individual exchanges one type of currency for the other. If done properly it can garner great ROI, and it is highly popular with investors worldwide. This is the reason why crooks tend to abuse this financial practice as there is always an influx of new traders who are ready to dabble in forex.

Fraudulent organizations set up numerous fake trading brokers with forex services. They provide unrealistic trading conditions, most commonly they advertise that trading is risk-free. These stealing sites practice social engineering to make the target invest more. Nevertheless, when they try to collect their funds, their account gets frozen.

CFD SCAM

This is the abbreviation of the term Contract For Difference (CFD), it refers to a financial practice which allows investors to capitalize on the shifts in the world market, without owning actual stocks or assets. This makes it more accessible to new investors, and it gained huge popularity worldwide. As a rule, when an investing opportunity becomes popular, scammers will try to exploit it.

Fraudulent parties will do their best to include CFD trading as an option in their fake trading platforms. The crooks employ social engineering and they manipulate figures so that a target would come to a conclusion the platform is wonderful and they should invest more. And so they do, falsely believing they are generating profit. Once they try to collect their money, to their shock they discover they can’t make any withdrawals.

Binary Options Scam

The binary option is the term for a quite straightforward financial practice. An individual forms a contract in which they declare the rise or fall of a price of a particular commodity at a specific point in time. Should the investor guess they gain profit. Since the outcome only depends on the win or lose dichotomy, it got the name Binary Options. This method of income has become quite popular due to its simplicity.

Knowing that a lot of people are searching for binary options online, scammers list them on their fraudulent trading sites. Like other trading options, this one is just in name only. The excited investor is drawn to no-risk high yield advertising, and they bite the bait. The broker will manipulate trading outcomes, to make the target think they are a binary options genius. This entices the victim to invest more and more funds. The moment they try to collect them back, they realize their assets are locked and unavailable.

Fake Investment Scam

Everyone is looking for a lucrative investment opportunity, but it’s often difficult to recognize what’s a legitimate once-in-a-lifetime opportunity and what's a too-good-to-be-true trap. Scammers will use this to their advantage and they will advertise get-rich-quick investment plans that are fail-proof and have no risk whatsoever.

These fake investment frauds usually end up being Ponzi schemes. The crooks entice victims with amazing offers. When they deposit their funds, they are not aware that the crooks have already stolen them.

Ponzi Schemes

With all the social media, YouTube, and social networks, the number of Ponzi Schemes has sky-rocketed. This is a very old type of scam that constantly gets recycled. Crooks sell investment packages that have astonishing ROI. However, as there is no outside source of revenue, and the scam doesn’t provide goods or services, the only income of funds comes from the investments of new arrivals into the scheme.

The victims who fall for Ponzi bait, usually get some sort of profit in the beginning. This makes them believe the system works and they are urged to invest more. What’s worse, they get enticed to recruit their family members and loved ones, which they do gladly. While there are new recruits, this type of pyramidal scam can function, but these scams have a short life. Inevitably they collapse leaving thousands if not millions of people scammed out of their funds and life savings.
